Jobs That Give Independence

24 hours a day, 7 days a week, Canine Companions for Independence® assistance dogs are there for their human partners with disabilities. Expertly trained to perform over 40 commands these dogs help children, veterans and adults with disabilities open doors, pick up dropped items and much more.

The Power of Independence

Canine Companions for Independence is the most highly acclaimed and accredited assistance dog organization in the United States.

Our unique and proven team training sets us apart by ensuring the match between dog and human is powerful and lasting.

Brandon & Service Dog Kary

Born without arms and legs, Brandon was matched with his first assistance dog at the North Central training facility more than 20 years ago. Since then, Brandon has had three Canine Companions service dogs by his side throughout the most important moments of his life.

“Working with the Canine Companions staff during my first Team Training made me super confident in public,” Brandon said. “I knew that with my dog by my side, I didn’t have anything to worry about.”

He says this changed his life and has helped him achieve his highest potential.

“My dogs have been with me every step of the way and help me live life to the fullest — from high school and college to my wedding and the birth of my daughter,” Brandon said.

Bob & Connie

When Bob and Connie decided to raise puppies for Canine Companions, they had no idea how rewarding the experience would be. Despite the hard work, they both believe it’s been one of the most fulfilling experiences of their lives.

"There's something about volunteering that is amazing on a human level," said Bob. “It’s incredible to see so many frowns turn into smiles — just because you have a dog with you.”

While they’ve treasured their experiences as volunteer puppy raisers, they say nothing compares to knowing they’re helping change someone’s life.

“When you see a dog you’ve raised helping someone gain independence, it’s all worth it,” said Connie. “It’s the ultimate reward.”

Melissa & Facility Dog Hud

Canine Companions’ facility dog, Hud II, works at Nationwide Children’s Hospital with Melissa. He’s trained to help in a variety of treatments, including physical medicine through gross-motor activities with patients.

Hud’s work helps hundreds of patients each year. Whether he’s assisting with therapies like walking or just being a friendly partner during treatment, Hud’s presence in the hospital has a lasting impact.

“He’s so gentle and intuitive to the needs of patients — they feel comfortable and safe with him,” said Melissa.
