Braden and Tando

Braden has spina bifida and has used a wheelchair his entire life. He loves taking care of his animals, including sheep and goats, adores fishing and enjoys building with Lego. As an active 11-year-old, Braden is always on the move. Before Canine Companions assistance dog Tando joined the family, Braden would get out of his chair when he dropped items, reach for toys off counters or have to ask for help.

"Braden is always reaching for things," says mom Jackie. Tando helps Braden be more independent by picking up dropped items and delivering them directly to Braden. Now Braden doesn’t have to ask his parents for help or get out of his wheelchair, thanks to his new best friend. "Braden hasn’t stopped smiling since we brought Tando home," Jackie says."Tando is an awesome dog."

Chrissy and Thel

U.S. Army veteran Chrissy first found out about Canine Companions for Independence at age 10 when her aunt was partnered with a Canine Companions assistance dog to help with multiple sclerosis. When Chrissy experienced a debilitating back injury while serving in the military, she knew she would turn to Canine Companions for her own assistance dog. But first she wanted to tackle the challenge of relearning how to walk.

Aquatic therapy helped Chrissy gain her function back, and the hours spent in the pool, along with other patients, led Chrissy to realize she wanted to be an occupational therapist. In 2016, Chrissy was matched with Canine Companions Service Dog Thel. "Getting down and getting back up is very difficult and slow," says Chrissy. "It’s wonderful to have a dog who can retrieve items off the ground and deliver them to me, both at home and on the job."

Chrissy is the rehabilitation manager at a skilled nursing facility, and Thel not only helps Chrissy with physical tasks, she also motivates patients during occupational therapy sessions with Chrissy. Thel helps Chrissy be more independent and enables her to do her job more easily. "Thel’s assistance has opened so many doors for me, but she’s also a help to countless others."

Jay and Hurley

Jay spent 20 years in the US Army as an interrogator and linguist fluent in Russian and Polish. His venerable career has given him a few stories to tell and a couple of scars, as well.

Jay’s spine was fractured in two places during an airborne operation. Subsequent testing revealed he also had a spine disorder. Twelve years and nine surgeries later, 22 of Jay’s 24 vertebrae are now fused - stabilized by rods, screws, and plates. Not only is Jay’s mobility limited, but he struggles with chronic pain. But after a nurse referred Jay to Canine Companions, everything changed.

Jay was matched with his first Canine Companions service dog, Timo, in 2008. Timo helped Jay become more independent and be in less pain. In 2017, Jay partnered with Service Dog Hurley to continue Timo’s amazing work. "I do not know how I would do many things without Hurley," says Jay. Hurley helps Jay with everyday tasks like picking up dropped items, opening doors and alerting people if Jay is in danger. Best of all, he provides Jay with constant companionship. Not only has his pain lessened because Hurley saves him from movements that would aggravate his spine, but Jay is a happier person with his expertly-trained service dog by his side.
